About Formula 2

FIA Formula 2 (F2) is a single-seater motorsport championship. It is considered a feeder series for Formula 1. Many young drivers compete in F2 to show their skills and try to move up to Formula 1.

  • Cars are similar for all teams (spec series) so driver skill matters a lot.
  • Races support Formula 1 race weekends at many tracks.
  • Drivers often are in their late teens or early twenties.

How it works

Each race weekend typically includes practice, qualifying and one or two races. The weekend format helps drivers practice racecraft and race strategy.

  1. Practice: Drivers test the car and learn the track.
  2. Qualifying: Determines the starting order for the main race (the feature race).
  3. Sprint / Feature races: Feature race is longer and often awards more points; sprint race is shorter.

Basic Rules

F2 has rules to keep competition fair and safe. Here are a few simple points:

  • All teams use similar chassis and engines (spec series).
  • Points are awarded to top finishers; the championship is decided by total points.
  • Pit stops and tyre choices can affect race strategy (in the feature race).
  • Drivers must follow safety flags and marshals' instructions at all times.
